Background

Born and raised in Chicago’s Chinatown area, I am a first-generation student who gained my first exposure in computer science through CS50. Discovering my passion for learning and the challenges that came with it, this experience inspired me to pursue Computer Science in college, where I continue to build on my skills and knowledge.

Daniel Schwen, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Where I am now

I'm currently a junior at the University of Wisconsin - Madison, pursuing a degree in Computer Science and Data Science. Most of my learning takes place outside of the classroom. Last Fall, I had my first experience with CodePath through their Web Development course. The experience helped me appreciate the value of hands-on projects and having deadlines. Since then, I’ve continued to take additional courses to build my repertoire of technical skills, and this Summer, I’m serving as a Tech Fellow for the Technical Interview Prep course to give back to the community.

Alongside, I'm working as a Technical Project Management intern at Cars Commerce, which was not something I expected, but I'm grateful to have an opportunity to build on both my soft and technical skills.

Where I'm going

My current summer internship has broadened my perspective, making me more open to exploring various career roles beyond software development. I’m eager to gain as much experience as possible before I graduate. In the remaining years of my college journey, I plan to immerse myself in new technologies, expand my professional network, and discover my strengths and weaknesses. I will continue to leverage learning opportunities like CodePath and headstarter to further my development.

Philosophy and Values

Try, Fail, Learn

When I first started my journey in web development, I wrote awful code. Then, I wrote slightly better code. When I tried iOS development with Swift, I wrote awful code again. Then, slightly better code. Learning computer science is this same iterative process, repeated over and over again.

Every Opportunity is a Hidden Gem

I began my journey in the restaurant industry—far from tech, but rich in lessons. No matter what you’re doing, even a small daily commitment to learning adds up over time. Embrace every opportunity as a chance to grow.

Enjoy the detour

Life rarely goes as planned. If you’re fixated on the straight path, you’ll miss the hidden treasures along the way. Embrace every detour! My goal was to be a software engineer, but as a technical project management intern, I’m discovering new strengths and aspirations. Sometimes the unexpected route is exactly what you need.
